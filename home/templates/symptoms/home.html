Sore Throat

One common home remedy is to gargle with warm salt water to reduce swelling and loosen mucus. Applying a cold cloth to the throat may also bring relief and combat the infection.

Over the counter medications include Advil, throat lozenges and other nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), which reduce swelling and inflammation.

Fevers

Fevers can be symptoms of a cold or infection, heat stroke, or dehydration.


Some home remedies for fevers include immersing your body in lukewarm water or putting an icepack on your forehead to lower your body temperature. You can also take vitamin C to boost your immune system. It is also important to drink plenty of fluids when you have a fever, as you may become dehydrated from sweating and chills.

Some over the counter medications to treat fevers are Advil, Asprin, and Aleve, which are all anti-inflammatories that reduce fever and pain. It is important to ensure that you do not take more than one of these medications concurrently, and drinking alcohol while on these medications may increase your risk of stomach bleeding. Another class of medication includes Tylenol and DayQuil, which contains Acetaminophen, which is another pain reliever and fever reducer. While you are taking acetaminophen, you cannot drink alcohol, as this combination can cause serious damage to your liver.

Headaches

Headaches can be symptoms of a cold or infection, lack of sleep, stress, dehydration or a multitude of other conditions.


Some home remedies for headaches include sleeping to alleviate stress and lack of sleep. It is also important to stay hydrated to prevent dehydration. A warm or cold compress can also be used on the neck to alleviate neck pain. Because headaches are a symptom of a multitude of conditions, it may be fruitful to try to treat the potential cause of the headache directly.
